SNOOKER

  1. Ronnie O'Sullivan

    2. Ronnie O’Sullivan has won a record 38 ranking titles and has also won more Triple Crown titles than any other player – six World Championships, seven Masters and seven UK Championships.

    O'Sullivan was the youngest player to win a professional ranking event, triumphing at the 1993 UK Championship aged 17 years and 358 days.

    He’s also made a record 30 consecutive World Championship appearances, from 1993-2020.

  2. Mark Selby

    3. 2019: Wins the English Open, crushing David Gilbert 9-1 in the final. Goes on to win the Scottish Open with a 9-6 final success over Jack Lisowski.

    2020: Reaches the semi-finals of the World Championship before a narrow 17-16 defeat against Ronnie O’Sullivan. Wins the European Masters, beating Martin Gould 9-8 in an exciting final. Wins the Scottish Open for the second consecutive year, thrashing Ronnie O’Sullivan 9-3.

    2021: Wins the World Championship for the fourth time with an exciting 18-15 defeat of Shaun Murphy in the final. Becomes only the fifth player to conquer the Crucible more than three times, joining Stephen Hendry, Ronnie O’Sullivan, Steve Davis and John Higgins. The £500,000 pay day is the biggest of his career.

    2022: Makes his fourth official 147 during the British Open.
